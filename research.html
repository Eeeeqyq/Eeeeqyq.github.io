(Ongoing) Transcriptomic Research Project — Independent Study

[Expected completion: November 2025] • Project Lead (Sep 2025 – Present)

This project examines immune transcriptomic differences in Beta-variant COVID-19 patients with and without prior Pfizer-BioNTech vaccination using RNA-seq data from GSE189039 (NCBI GEO). I focus on building a reproducible pipeline that begins with quality control and alignment, continues through quantification and normalization, and culminates in differential expression and pathway enrichment analyses. The work emphasizes documentation and transparency so that figures and statistics are fully traceable from the raw data to final results. Beyond the technical goals, the motivation is to clarify how vaccination shapes early antiviral signaling and longer-term immune programs following infection. By comparing vaccinated and unvaccinated cohorts across multiple post-infection timepoints, I evaluate whether vaccine-primed responses—such as interferon signaling and JAK-STAT pathway activation—are consistently elevated and whether they correlate with protective mechanisms discussed in recent literature. The broader aim is to practice careful scientific reasoning: making specific, testable claims, reporting limitations, and outlining next steps that could strengthen conclusions.

What I did

  • Implemented analysis in Python (pandas, matplotlib) and R (DESeq2, GSEA).
  • Ran differential gene expression and pathway enrichment with clear QC checkpoints.
  • Identified up-regulated interferon and JAK-STAT signaling as vaccine-primed responses.
  • Created reproducible notebooks and exportable figures for communication.

Next steps include validating results on additional public datasets, refining batch-effect handling, and building a small web gallery to host interactive figures for broader sharing.
